# Learner's confusion: what forces the ∞-tower, and homotopy vs identity (2026-07-14)

While reviewing, the learner got stuck on the "infinite" part of ∞-groupoids. Three tangled sub-questions: (1) if two paths = two ways of being identical, why would we ever *need* to claim two paths are themselves identical? (2) Was that higher-level reasoning even needed in the Binary = Bool example? (3) "Maybe they're homotopies but not really identical" — what is the relationship between a homotopy between paths and an identity of paths?

Strong conceptual signal; the learner's own guesses were mostly right and just needed confirming + sharpening. Ties to [[0003-terminology-precision]] (wants the precise object), [[0005-unprovable-vs-false]], and the Lesson 3/4 material.

## The answer given (bank so later lessons stay consistent)

1. **You never impose higher identity; the tower is the space of questions you MAY ask, and h-level is how high it stays nontrivial.** Not a demand that everything be proven equal at every level. Most types go flat fast (sets). Reframe led with this.
2. **What generates the climb:** doing algebra on paths. Compose `p ∙ q` and ask if `(p∙q)∙r = p∙(q∙r)` — two paths of the same type, so the question lives one level up. Groupoid operations generate the next level, forever = the ∞.
3. **Binary = Bool didn't need it (learner was right):** Bool/Binary are sets (h-level 2), tower flat above paths. But the *universe* is a groupoid: `refl ≠ ua not` in `Bool = Bool` (we do NOT claim they're identical; the disequality is what makes the universe a groupoid). One level up (`refl = ua not`) is a mere prop (empty here) and everything above is trivial. "∞ is the capacity, the h-level is the actual height." Loops back to Lesson 3.
4. **Homotopy IS identity (the crux):** in HoTT a homotopy between paths p, q *is* an inhabitant of `p = q`. No separate "really identical." "Identical" at every level means "path-connected one level up," never "literally the same token" — same as `a = b` at the bottom never meaning "same symbol." The only stricter notion is judgmental `≡` (Lesson 4), which IS "literally the same by computation" and is separate. So the learner's "homotopic but not really identical" dichotomy dissolves: it exists for `≡`, not for `=`.

## Implications for later lessons
- The ∞-tower is under-motivated if presented as "and it continues forever." Present it as *generated by operations on paths* (associativity/coherence) and *bounded by h-level*. Reuse the "space of questions, not a demand" framing.
- **HITs lesson (#8)** and any 2-path reasoning: make homotopy = identity explicit up front; the learner will otherwise keep looking for a stricter "real" identity behind it. Anchor "identical means path-connected one level up" and contrast with `≡`.
- The `refl ≠ ua not`, then trivial-above example is a strong, owned anchor for "how tall" — reuse it.
- Possible short lesson between #5 and #6, or a beat inside #6/#7: "coherence — why composition being associative is a *higher path*, and why sets don't care." Decide with learner (loose IOU, see [[NOTES.md]]).
